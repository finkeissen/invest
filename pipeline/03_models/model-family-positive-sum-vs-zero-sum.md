# Model Family: Positive-Sum vs Zero-Sum vs Destructive

## Purpose

This model family classifies whether an action or strategy creates net value, merely transfers it, or destroys it while appearing locally beneficial.

This distinction is required for both person-level and group-level evaluation.

---

## Core objective

To distinguish between:
- genuinely productive gains,
- redistributive gains,
- destructive gains that are only locally attractive.

---

## Main variables

- gross value created
- value transferred
- value destroyed
- collateral damage
- time horizon of effects
- outsider burden
- repair burden

---

## Key mechanisms

### Positive-sum

The action increases future capacity, welfare, resilience, or optionality without requiring equivalent hidden losses elsewhere.

### Zero-sum

The action mainly reallocates gain from one actor or group to another.

### Destructive

The action creates local advantage while reducing total welfare, trust, resilience, or future capacity.

---

## Classification warning

Many strategies change class depending on time horizon.

A short-run win may become destructive once delayed harms, repair cost, and trust erosion are counted.

---

## Typical outputs

- strategic class
- confidence level
- delayed-harm warning
- externalization warning
- horizon-sensitive reclassification note

---

## Main failure modes

- calling extraction value creation,
- ignoring delayed destruction,
- evaluating only winners,
- hiding losses outside the reporting boundary.
