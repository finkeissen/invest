# Model Family: Group Distribution

## Purpose

This model family represents how gains, costs, and risks are distributed across members and affected outsiders.

The aim is to prevent group-level success claims from hiding person-level losses.

---

## Core objective

To answer:
- who benefits,
- who pays,
- how concentrated the burden is,
- whether the arrangement remains acceptable,
- whether the group result depends on hidden sacrifice.

---

## Main variables

- member classes
- benefit distribution
- burden distribution
- decision power distribution
- repair capacity
- voice and exit options
- outsider harms

---

## Key mechanisms

### 1. Concentrated burden detection

A group may appear successful while imposing steep cost on a visible minority.

### 2. Hidden maintenance extraction

Care work, emotional labor, and administrative labor may be systematically undercounted.

### 3. Legitimacy feedback

Persistent unfairness may degrade trust, retention, and long-run coordination quality.

---

## Typical outputs

- distribution profile
- concentrated harm warning
- fairness or legitimacy warning
- repair obligation record
- unstable success warning

---

## Main failure modes

- averaging away victims,
- ignoring outsider costs,
- treating silence as consent,
- confusing short-run performance with durable group success.
