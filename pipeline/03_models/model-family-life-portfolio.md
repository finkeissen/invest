# Model Family: Life Portfolio

## Purpose

This model family represents how a person or group allocates scarce resources across multiple life domains under uncertainty.

It is the central model family for turning broad investment philosophy into practical planning.

---

## Core objective

To model how actors choose, sequence, and review investments across domains such as:
- health,
- education,
- work,
- savings,
- relationships,
- meaning,
- institutional participation,
- optionality.

---

## Main variables

- current condition by domain
- declared goals
- constraints
- available resources
- headwinds
- tailwinds
- dependencies between actions
- expected gains
- expected downside
- maintenance load
- review cadence

---

## Key mechanisms

### 1. Multi-domain allocation

Actors distribute finite resources across domains rather than solving a one-domain optimization problem.

### 2. Dependency-sensitive sequencing

Some investments only perform well when earlier prerequisites are already stable.

### 3. Maintenance pressure

Existing capacities decay unless maintenance is funded.

### 4. Overconcentration risk

Excessive investment in one domain may weaken the whole portfolio.

---

## Typical outputs

- ordered action portfolio
- neglected-domain warning
- overload warning
- dependency map
- review schedule
- expected trade-off profile

---

## Main failure modes

- sacrificing prerequisites for upside,
- hidden maintenance starvation,
- overloading attention and energy,
- pretending all gains are commensurable,
- ignoring effects on close others.
