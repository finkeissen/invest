# Model Family: Risk and Reversibility

## Purpose

This model family represents downside, uncertainty, lock-in, and recovery potential.

A practical investment framework must not only estimate upside. It must distinguish reversible mistakes from irreversible damage.

---

## Core objective

To classify actions by:
- downside size,
- downside concentration,
- reversibility,
- recovery time,
- uncertainty level,
- optionality preserved or destroyed.

---

## Main variables

- probability of failure
- severity of failure
- reversibility class
- recovery cost
- time to detect failure
- optionality retained
- dependence on optimistic assumptions

---

## Key distinctions

### Reversible vs. irreversible

A wrong but reversible action is often preferable to a slightly better-looking but irreversible one.

### Fragile upside vs. robust adequacy

Some actions offer large upside but collapse under small shocks. Others offer modest gain but strong resilience.

### Visible risk vs. hidden risk

A stable-looking action may hide health, trust, or maintenance risk that arrives later.

---

## Typical outputs

- risk class
- reversibility class
- recovery estimate
- lock-in warning
- optionality score or classification
- stop-rule recommendation

---

## Main failure modes

- chasing upside under hidden fragility,
- misclassifying irreversible commitments as ordinary bets,
- ignoring recovery capacity,
- confusing low volatility with low risk.
