# Coverage

## Purpose

This file defines the initial world boundary for Invest. The aim is not full completeness, but a disciplined and expandable starting scope for practical investment reasoning.

The repository should support at least three overlapping coverage layers:
1. person-level life planning
2. group and institutional investment
3. geopolitical and market investment

## A. Human life coverage

The first version should include the main domains in which persons commonly invest.

### Life domains in scope
- health
- education
- work and income
- savings and financial protection
- housing
- family and relationships
- time structure and habits
- community belonging
- meaning, worldview, and spiritual practice

### Typical exclusions for early versions
- exhaustive personality modeling
- full psychotherapy frameworks
- total life prediction

## B. Group and institutional coverage

The first version should include collective units that shape personal outcomes.

### Collective entities in scope
- households
- families
- local communities
- learning groups
- firms
- schools
- hospitals
- religious communities
- municipalities

### Group dimensions in scope
- trust
- coordination
- legitimacy
- capability
- resilience
- collateral damage to members and outsiders

## C. Geopolitical and market coverage

The first version should prioritize entities that are both:
1. strategically important in geopolitical interaction, and
2. economically or financially relevant for downstream market interpretation.

### States and jurisdictions in scope
- United States
- China
- European Union and major EU members
- United Kingdom
- Russia
- Japan
- India
- Taiwan
- South Korea
- Saudi Arabia
- Iran
- Israel
- Turkey

### Blocs and alliances in scope
- EU
- NATO
- G7
- BRICS
- OPEC+
- ASEAN

### Sectoral domains in scope
- energy
- semiconductors
- defense and aerospace
- logistics and shipping
- critical minerals
- compute and cloud infrastructure

## Inclusion rule

An entity or domain belongs in scope if it meets at least one of the following:
- it strongly shapes future options for persons or groups
- it compounds over time
- it can create major downside if neglected
- it strongly mediates geopolitical or market outcomes
- it is needed to evaluate collateral damage or distribution of gain

## Exclusion rule

Keep out domains that add surface area without improving decision quality.

The system should prefer disciplined scope over decorative completeness.

## Important boundary note

The repository must not assume that household-level or person-level investment is out of scope by default.

That would be acceptable for a narrow geopolitical system, but not for Invest as a general practical framework.
