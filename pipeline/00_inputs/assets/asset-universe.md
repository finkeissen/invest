# Asset Universe

## Purpose

This file defines the initial set of assets and capital forms that connect investment decisions to future outcomes.

The universe is broader than market instruments. It should support practical reasoning for persons, groups, and geopolitical systems.

## Asset family A: Financial assets

### Sovereign and rates exposures
- US Treasuries
- German Bunds
- selected emerging-market sovereigns
- inflation-linked sovereign debt

### Equities and equity proxies
- broad US equity index
- Europe equity index
- China or Greater China proxy
- Japan equity index
- defense basket
- semiconductor basket
- energy producers basket
- industrials and logistics basket

### Commodities
- crude oil
- natural gas proxy
- uranium proxy where relevant
- copper
- gold

### FX
- USD broad strength proxy
- EUR/USD
- USD/CNY or offshore proxy
- JPY crosses where strategic risk-off behavior matters

### Credit and funding stress proxies
- investment-grade spread proxy
- high-yield spread proxy
- cross-currency funding stress proxy

- reserve adequacy
- alliance credibility
- critical-mineral access

## Selection rule

Assets in scope should satisfy at least one of the following:
- they absorb shocks quickly
- they preserve future optionality
- they enable compounding
- they protect against catastrophic loss
- they proxy personal, group, or strategic resilience
- they are useful validation anchors for scenarios

## Important warning

Do not assume all assets are directly commensurable.

Some gains can be compared only within bounded contexts. For example, health, money, trust, and meaning should not automatically be reduced to one scalar score.
