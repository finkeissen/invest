# Indicator Catalog

## Purpose

This file lists the first indicator families that should anchor world-state description in Invest.

## Family A: Personal vitality indicators

- sleep quality
- resting energy and fatigue profile
- physical capacity
- recovery time
- mental stability
- concentration quality
- addiction risk or dependency markers

## Family B: Personal capability indicators

- literacy and comprehension
- skill progression
- completed training milestones
- work output quality
- judgment quality proxy
- learning velocity
- schedule control

## Family C: Household stability indicators

- cash buffer months
- debt burden
- housing stability
- caregiving load
- conflict frequency
- food and energy affordability
- emergency resilience

## Family D: Group coordination indicators

- trust level
- attendance reliability
- conflict resolution success
- leadership legitimacy
- communication quality
- volunteer retention
- shared-goal clarity

## Family E: Institutional health indicators

- staff continuity
- process reliability
- service quality
- mission adherence
- budget sustainability
- public trust proxy
- audit or governance quality

## Family F: Macro and policy indicators

- CPI or inflation rate
- policy rates
- sovereign yield levels and spreads
- industrial production
- unemployment
- fiscal balance or debt-stress proxy

## Family G: External balance and trade indicators

- trade balance
- export concentration
- import dependency on strategic sectors
- shipping or freight stress proxies
- energy import exposure

## Family H: Political and geopolitical indicators

- election calendar proximity
- cabinet or leadership turnover
- sanctions status
- alliance cohesion proxy
- defense-spending trend
- conflict escalation level

## Family I: Market indicators

- equity index level and drawdown
- implied volatility proxy
- credit spreads
- FX pressure
- commodity price level
- sector-relative performance

## Family J: Strategic capacity indicators

- semiconductor production capacity proxy
- energy spare capacity proxy
- critical-mineral dependency
- defense production lead-time proxy
- reserve adequacy or strategic-buffer indicator

## Data design rule

Each concrete indicator file or dataset should record:
- what the indicator measures
- why it matters
- frequency
- lag structure
- likely source class
- known failure modes
- downstream use

## Warning

Indicators are not reality itself.

Some of the most important human goods are only imperfectly measurable. The model should treat low-measurement domains with humility rather than ignoring them.
