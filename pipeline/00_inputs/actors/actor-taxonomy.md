# Actor Taxonomy

## Purpose

This file defines the initial actor classes that Invest should support before detailed agent design begins.

The taxonomy is intentionally multi-level. It should support practical investment reasoning for single persons, groups of persons, and large strategic systems.

## Level 1: Personal actors

### 1. Individual persons

Examples:
- student
- worker
- parent
- entrepreneur
- patient
- retiree

Typical concerns:
- survival and safety
- health and energy
- education and skill growth
- income and savings
- family obligations
- time allocation
- meaning and life direction

### 2. Households and families

Examples:
- single-person household
- couple
- family with children
- multigenerational household

Typical concerns:
- budget stability
- housing
- care work
- education of children
- health resilience
- conflict management
- intergenerational transfer

## Level 2: Group actors

### 3. Communities and local groups

Examples:
- neighborhood group
- learning circle
- mutual-aid network
- religious community
- association

Typical concerns:
- trust
- cohesion
- coordination quality
- shared norms
- local resilience
- education and support structures

### 4. Firms and productive organizations

Examples:
- small business
- strategic producer
- logistics platform
- software company
- industrial manufacturer

Typical concerns:
- survival
- capital expenditure timing
- labor quality
- supply security
- regulation
- market access
- execution discipline

### 5. Institutions

Examples:
- school
- university
- hospital
- regulator
- charity
- church
- municipality

Typical concerns:
- mission integrity
- legitimacy
- service quality
- staffing
- funding stability
- institutional trust
- long-term stewardship

## Level 3: Macro and geopolitical actors

### 6. State actors

Examples:
- sovereign governments
- ministries
- national security establishments

Typical concerns:
- regime stability
- external security
- industrial capacity
- fiscal room
- diplomatic leverage

### 7. Monetary and regulatory institutions

Examples:
- central banks
- finance ministries
- market regulators
- trade authorities

Typical concerns:
- inflation
- financial stability
- capital mobility
- sanctions enforcement
- market functioning

### 8. Alliances and blocs

Examples:
- NATO
- EU
- G7
- BRICS
- OPEC+

Typical concerns:
- coordination
- burden sharing
- credibility
- bargaining power
- bloc cohesion

### 9. Non-state disruptive actors

Examples:
- militias
- cyber groups
- criminal networks
- extremist organizations

Typical concerns:
- disruption
- leverage through asymmetry
- coercion
- illicit finance
- institutional erosion

## Modeling rule

Actor classes should not be treated as morally equivalent or structurally identical.

The system may compare them functionally, but must still preserve:
- different constraints
- different legitimate goals
- different permissible actions
- different scales of collateral damage

## Downstream use

This taxonomy feeds:
- agent design
- incentive mapping
- conflict analysis
- investment opportunity search
- investment risk and damage assessment
