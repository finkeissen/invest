# Life Portfolio Theory

## Purpose

This document translates the broad investment philosophy of Invest-GT into a practical portfolio framework for real persons and groups.

A life portfolio is the ordered set of investments by which an actor tries to build, preserve, and improve future condition across multiple domains.

---

## Why a portfolio view is needed

People do not invest only in one thing.

They simultaneously allocate scarce resources across:
- health,
- education,
- work and income,
- financial reserves,
- relationships,
- meaning and orientation,
- institutional and civic commitments.

A portfolio view prevents over-optimization of one domain at the expense of the rest.

---

## Portfolio construction sequence

A practical life-portfolio process should proceed in this order:

1. define goals,
2. define constraints,
3. define current condition,
4. identify available domains,
5. generate candidate investments,
6. score gain, downside, and prerequisites,
7. sort by sequence and dependency,
8. choose a manageable portfolio,
9. define execution rhythm,
10. review and revise.

---

## Core portfolio domains

A minimal domain taxonomy for life portfolios:
- health
- mental stability
- education and skill
- work and income
- financial resilience
- relationships and trust
- meaning and spiritual orientation
- character and habit structure
- civic and institutional participation
- strategic optionality

Not every actor will weight these equally.

---

## Constraints

Any executable portfolio must explicitly represent constraints such as:
- money,
- time,
- energy,
- health,
- dependents,
- geography,
- social obligations,
- institutional constraints,
- legal constraints,
- moral constraints.

---

## Portfolio heuristics

Useful default heuristics include:
- protect basic stability before pursuing upside,
- remove severe headwinds early,
- prefer compounding investments when prerequisites are met,
- do not buy upside by destroying core capacities,
- preserve optionality where uncertainty is high,
- include maintenance, not only growth,
- keep review points explicit.

---

## Execution and review

A portfolio is not complete when written down. It becomes real only when paired with:
- a sequence,
- a cadence,
- stop rules,
- escalation rules,
- revision triggers,
- evidence about what is or is not working.

This is the practical bridge between theory and life execution.
