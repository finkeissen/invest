# Scope of Invest-GT

## Purpose

Invest-GT is a domain-specific research and simulation repository within the broader Finkeissen research architecture.

It applies the upstream epistemic discipline of admissibility, provenance, constraint-based processing, and auditability to the domain of investment-relevant world modeling, hypothesis construction, simulation, and allocation-oriented reasoning.

This repository is therefore not a general theory of everything, not a universal decision engine, and not a free-form speculative forecasting system.

Its scope must remain explicit.

---

## Relation to the broader architecture

The broader Finkeissen architecture defines the epistemic regime under which repositories are interpreted and processed.

In that wider setting:

- admissibility precedes claims
- provenance matters
- context binding is mandatory
- unresolved or inadmissible artifacts must not be silently upgraded into accepted outputs

Invest-GT inherits these constraints.

It must therefore be read as a downstream application layer for a specific problem domain rather than as an autonomous epistemic system.

For architectural embedding, see `docs/finkeissen-architecture.md`.

---

## What "investment" means here

In this repository, "investment" is used in a broad but still disciplined sense.

It does not refer only to buying and selling financial instruments.

It refers more generally to the allocation of limited resources under uncertainty across time, based on explicit assumptions about the world, causal structure, constraints, and expected outcomes.

Relevant resources can include, depending on context:

- capital
- time
- attention
- energy
- organizational capacity
- political leverage
- strategic optionality
- institutional trust
- industrial capability
- technological development capacity

This means that investment can occur at multiple levels, including:

- individual actors
- firms
- sectors
- states
- alliances
- infrastructures
- civilizational or long-range strategic systems

However, this breadth does not make the scope unlimited.

The term "investment" remains anchored to questions of allocation, commitment, trade-offs, uncertainty, and consequences over time.

---

## Primary focus

The primary focus of Invest-GT is the structured study of investment-relevant consequences of world developments.

This includes, in particular:

- geopolitical developments
- macroeconomic developments
- industrial shifts
- institutional changes
- technological dynamics
- supply and infrastructure dependencies
- policy and regulation effects
- strategic competition between actors
- scenario-dependent changes in risk, opportunity, and allocation logic

The repository is therefore interested in how world conditions shape decision spaces for allocative behavior.

---

## Core object of study

The core object of study is not "the market" in isolation.

The core object of study is the relation between:

1. changing world conditions
2. structured hypotheses about those changes
3. scenario formation
4. simulation of consequences
5. decision-relevant allocation implications

This means the repository is fundamentally about:

- world modeling under constraints
- explicit hypothesis formation
- structured uncertainty handling
- auditable simulation
- interpretable consequence analysis

Financial markets may appear as one downstream expression of this process, but they are not the only valid target layer.

## In scope

The following areas are in scope for this repository when handled under explicit epistemic and methodological constraints.

### 1. World-state representation

Representations of relevant world conditions, including:

- political context
- institutional context
- economic context
- industrial context
- technological context
- conflict and security context
- resource and infrastructure context

### 2. Hypothesis construction

Explicit, versioned, falsifiable hypotheses about:

- causal mechanisms
- directional effects
- actor behavior
- regime changes
- second-order effects
- market and non-market consequences

### 3. Scenario construction

Structured scenarios that define:

- relevant actors
- triggers
- assumptions
- constraints
- temporal structure
- possible pathways
- uncertainty boundaries

### 4. Agent and model design

Formal or semi-formal actor representations and model components used to simulate:

- incentives
- constraints
- reactions
- interactions
- adaptation
- allocation behavior

### 5. Simulation and consequence analysis

Simulations intended to examine:

- plausible consequence structures
- strategic sensitivities
- allocation implications
- robustness under uncertainty
- dependence on assumptions

### 6. Reporting and auditability

Production of outputs that remain:

- traceable
- reproducible
- inspectable
- interpretable
- tied back to explicit assumptions and inputs

---

## Explicitly out of scope

The following are out of scope unless the repository is later redefined explicitly.

### 1. Free-floating claims

Claims without clear contextual binding, provenance, or admissibility status are out of scope.

If a statement cannot be tied to a concrete problem context, it is epistemically inadmissible under the broader architecture.

### 2. Purely rhetorical macro commentary

This repository is not a vehicle for unconstrained opinion writing, broad commentary, or stylistic forecasting language detached from explicit structures.

### 3. Trading tips and informal market calls

The repository is not primarily a newsletter, signal feed, discretionary trading log, or casual prediction engine.

Short-term trading content may only appear if it is structurally derived from admissible hypotheses, explicit inputs, and reproducible modeling steps.

### 4. Total-life philosophy as repository core

Questions of life, maturity, sincerity, existential orientation, or broader philosophical reflection may be related to the larger Finkeissen ecosystem, but they are not the operational core of this repository.

They should not silently replace the domain focus of Invest-GT.

### 5. Unbounded general intelligence claims

This repository does not claim to solve general intelligence, universal knowledge, or unrestricted truth production.

It is a constrained domain repository.

### 6. Black-box output generation

Opaque outputs that cannot be reconstructed, traced, or audited are out of scope as accepted results.

## Domain emphasis

Although "investment" is broad here, Invest-GT is not equally about everything.

The dominant emphasis is on investment-relevant interpretation of:

- geopolitics
- macroeconomics
- industrial structure
- technology and infrastructure
- strategic behavior of large actors
- long-horizon allocation consequences

This means the repository is closer to a structured geopolitical-macro allocation research system than to a generic personal finance or retail investing project.

---

## Unit of analysis

Depending on the task, the unit of analysis may vary.

Valid units of analysis can include:

- an actor
- a region
- a sector
- an industry
- a supply chain
- a strategic dependency
- a policy regime
- a conflict situation
- an asset class
- a portfolio logic
- a scenario path

The acceptable unit of analysis is determined by the concrete problem context, not by a fixed ideological preference.

---

## Time horizon

The repository is not restricted to one fixed time horizon.

Depending on context, valid horizons can include:

- short-term reactions
- medium-term transitions
- long-term structural changes

However, the time horizon must always be made explicit.

No projection should be interpreted without a defined horizon.

---

## Methodological consequences of this scope

Because the scope is broad but constrained, the repository must avoid two opposite errors.

### Error 1: collapse into narrow finance-only thinking

If the repository treats investment only as asset-price movement, it loses the broader allocative reality that often drives those movements.

### Error 2: dissolve into unlimited world-theory

If the repository expands into unconstrained philosophy, social theory, or general commentary, it loses operational sharpness and becomes structurally weak.

The correct scope is therefore:

- broader than finance-only
- narrower than general world explanation
- explicitly tied to allocation under uncertainty

---

## Canonical reading rule

A topic belongs in this repository only if it can be connected to all of the following:

1. a concrete problem context
2. an allocation or investment-relevant question
3. explicit assumptions
4. a traceable reasoning structure
5. an auditable artifact path

If these conditions are absent, the topic should not be treated as a valid core artifact of Invest-GT.

---

## Examples of valid repository questions

Examples of questions that fit the repository include:

- How could a geopolitical conflict alter long-term capital allocation logic across sectors and regions?
- Which hypotheses about industrial policy shifts are strong enough to justify scenario modeling?
- How should strategic dependencies be represented in a simulation of allocation responses?
- What investment-relevant consequences follow if a policy regime changes under explicit assumptions?
- How robust is an allocation conclusion when specific macro assumptions are perturbed?

These are structurally valid because they connect world conditions, hypotheses, consequences, and allocation logic.

---

## Examples of invalid or weakly scoped questions

Examples of weakly scoped questions include:

- What is the future of everything?
- Which country is best in general?
- What will markets do next, without assumptions or context?
- What is the meaning of life as an investment thesis?
- Which opinion sounds most convincing?

These fail because they are insufficiently bound, insufficiently operationalized, or insufficiently admissible.

---

## Boundaries with related repositories

Some topics may touch nearby repositories in the wider architecture.

As a rule of thumb:

- upstream admissibility rules belong to the broader architecture, not primarily to Invest-GT
- domain-specific world modeling, hypothesis formation, simulation, and allocation analysis belong here
- artifacts that fail admissibility or cannot be responsibly advanced should not be normalized inside this repository

Invest-GT is therefore not the place to weaken upstream epistemic discipline.

It is the place to apply it to a difficult domain.
