# Collateral Damage and Externalities

## Purpose

This document gives Invest-GT an explicit place for dealing with harms, side effects, distributional conflicts, and tragic trade-offs.

A framework that speaks about gain without making harm legible will drift into moral fog.

---

## Core claim

No serious investment framework is complete unless it records:
- who benefits,
- who pays,
- who carries risk,
- who is excluded,
- who can object,
- what damage is tolerated.

---

## Main distinctions

### Positive-sum, zero-sum, and destructive outcomes

An outcome may:
- create net new value,
- redistribute value,
- destroy value while appearing locally beneficial.

These cases should not be conflated.

### Direct and indirect harms

Harms may be:
- direct,
- delayed,
- distributed,
- hidden,
- reputational,
- institutional,
- intergenerational.

### Concentrated and diffuse effects

A group-level improvement can still be morally or strategically defective if the gains are diffuse while the harms are concentrated on identifiable persons.

---

## Lesser-evil logic

Some choices involve real tragedy rather than clean optimization.

When all available options involve some damage, records should state:
- what harms attach to each option,
- why the chosen option was judged less damaging,
- what mitigation is planned,
- what repair obligations remain.

---

## Minimum review questions

Every major investment or strategy should answer:
- Who gains?
- Who loses?
- Is the loss avoidable?
- Is the loss reversible?
- Is there a lower-harm path?
- Are the affected persons visible in the record?
- Does the gain rely on moral externalization?
