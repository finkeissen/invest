# Overview

## Purpose

**Invest-GT** explores how political and geopolitical dynamics shape strategic investment behavior, and how those investment responses feed back into institutions, incentives, and market conditions.

The framework is designed for structured experimentation rather than single-shot prediction.

## Working definition of investment

In this project, **investment** means the deliberate allocation of scarce resources toward future value under uncertainty. Depending on the model, those resources may include:

- capital
- time
- organizational capacity
- policy attention
- strategic credibility
- coordination effort
- health capacity
- educational effort
- social trust
- meaning-bearing discipline

This broader definition is useful when modeling persons, groups, institutions, governments, firms, and markets within the same simulation environment.

## Why this project exists

Many real-world dynamics sit between disciplinary boundaries:

- politics changes incentives, constraints, and narrative expectations
- markets react to those shifts through pricing, positioning, and capital allocation
- institutions respond to market outcomes, social pressure, and strategic rivalries

Traditional models often capture only one slice of that loop. Invest-GT is meant to provide a modular way to represent the loop explicitly.

## Core idea

> Separate models first. Validate them independently. Define interaction rules explicitly. Then integrate them into a shared runtime.

That approach helps with:

- interpretability
- debugging
- versioning assumptions
- historical comparison
- disciplined hypothesis building

## Scope

Invest-GT is best understood as a **research framework** for:

- political-risk scenarios
- macro stress testing
- strategic interaction models
- exploratory multi-agent simulations
- comparative hypothesis evaluation
- personal and collective life-portfolio reasoning

It should not be treated as a production trading system or a source of direct investment advice.


## Additional practical commitments

The broader investment view implies several practical commitments:

- person-level and group-level evaluation should remain distinct before aggregation
- gain claims should identify beneficiaries, costs, and harms
- health, education, and meaning are valid investment domains
- a mature version of the repo should support the compilation of life goals into executable portfolios of action
