# Ecosystem Position

## Why this document exists

This repository is intended to stand on its own.

It should not depend on hidden companion repositories, undocumented external scaffolding, or implicit conceptual layers that exist somewhere else. The core logic, structure, and reasoning of the project should be understandable from within this repository itself.

At the same time, Invest-GT does not emerge in a vacuum.

Several adjacent traditions already expand the meaning of investment beyond narrow financial return. They treat time, education, health, social relationships, institutions, trust, or collective capacity as forms of capital or as domains of long-term value creation.

This document therefore serves two purposes:

- to clarify that the repository is conceptually self-supporting
- to place Invest-GT in relation to nearby approaches without collapsing into them

## Repository independence

Invest-GT should remain usable and interpretable as a self-contained framework.

That means:

- no required hidden dependencies on other repositories
- no external conceptual layer that must be known in advance
- no silent outsourcing of core definitions or design logic
- no assumption that key ideas live somewhere else

This does not mean the repository ignores the wider intellectual landscape. It means that outside influences, references, and related traditions should remain optional context rather than structural prerequisites.

## Nearby traditions

### Broad capital frameworks

Some existing frameworks already treat wealth or capital as more than money alone.

These approaches often distinguish several forms of capital such as human, social, natural, cultural, political, intellectual, or built capital. Their main contribution is to show that long-term prosperity depends on more than financial assets and that investment in one domain often affects others.

This is close to Invest-GT in several ways:

- it broadens the meaning of investment
- it treats multiple capital forms as relevant
- it makes interdependence visible
- it resists reduction of all value to money

However, many such approaches are centered on description, sustainability, or community development rather than on a stricter decision framework.

### Life-design and self-investment approaches

Another nearby family of approaches treats life as something that can be designed, shaped, or invested in.

These approaches often focus on questions such as:

- how to spend time well
- how to align work and life
- how to build a meaningful future
- how to invest in skills, health, and relationships

This overlaps strongly with Invest-GT at the personal level.

Invest-GT agrees that health, education, recovery, skills, and relationships can all be treated as investments if they improve future conditions for action and life.

The difference is that Invest-GT tries to make these choices more explicitly comparable through constraints, trade-offs, risk, sequencing, and portfolio logic rather than leaving them mainly at the level of reflection or advice.

### Systems and impact frameworks

Some systems-oriented approaches in philanthropy, impact investing, policy, and strategic planning already think in terms of:

- multi-level effects
- delayed consequences
- feedback loops
- distribution effects
- longer time horizons

These are close to Invest-GT because they recognize that direct return is not the only relevant outcome and that second-order effects matter.

Invest-GT shares this orientation, but differs in scope. It is not only concerned with institutional capital deployment. It also asks how persons, households, groups, and institutions build portfolios of action under uncertainty.

## What Invest-GT adds

### 1. A broad functional definition of investment

Invest-GT uses a deliberately broad concept of investment:

> the deliberate allocation of scarce resources in expectation of future gain under uncertainty

This includes not only money, but also:

- time
- energy
- attention
- trust
- health
- education
- coordination effort
- legitimacy
- institutional capacity
- other scarce and decision-relevant resources

This makes it possible to compare financial, personal, social, and strategic decisions within one general framework without pretending they are all identical.

### 2. Explicit distinction between persons and groups

A central feature of Invest-GT is the strict distinction between:

- outcomes for individual persons
- outcomes for groups, collectives, institutions, or societies

Many frameworks speak loosely about collective benefit or social good without clearly tracking who benefits, who pays, and whether gains are symmetrically distributed.

Invest-GT treats this as a first-order problem.

A group may appear to gain while some individuals absorb severe losses. In such cases, group-level success can conceal redistribution, coercion, exclusion, or damage transfer.

For that reason, Invest-GT insists on evaluating both levels separately before combining them.

### 3. Visible trade-offs and collateral damage

Invest-GT treats trade-offs as unavoidable and therefore worth making explicit.

It does not assume that all gains can be reduced to one common metric. It also does not assume that aggregate benefit automatically justifies local damage.

This leads to strong emphasis on:

- downside
- reversibility
- maintenance burdens
- externalities
- collateral damage
- asymmetry
- lesser-evil reasoning under constraint
- positive-sum vs. zero-sum vs. destructive dynamics

One aim is to preserve moral and strategic friction rather than smoothing it away.

### 4. Portfolio logic beyond finance

Invest-GT treats life and group strategy as portfolio problems rather than isolated decisions.

A person does not only make one choice. A person allocates scarce resources across domains such as:

- survival
- health
- learning
- work
- family
- resilience
- recovery
- reputation
- optionality
- long-term projects

Groups do something similar at another scale.

This means the relevant question is often not only “is this a good action?” but also:

- where does this belong in the portfolio?
- what should come first?
- what dependencies exist?
- what risks does it hedge?
- what opportunities does it open or close?
- what does it preserve?
- who gains and who loses?

### 5. Operational orientation

Invest-GT is not only a philosophical vocabulary.

The repository is structured toward operationalization through explicit layers such as inputs, hypotheses, agents, models, runtime, simulations, tests, and reports. That architectural and pipeline orientation is already a visible part of the repository itself.

Its ambition is not merely to name important concepts, but eventually to support explicit life portfolios, action sequencing, and person/group evaluations in a more disciplined way.

## What Invest-GT is not

Invest-GT is not a claim that older approaches are useless or obsolete.

It is also not a claim that all life questions can be solved through optimization.

It is not:

- a replacement for empirical research
- a universal moral calculus
- a finished decision engine
- a complete theory of society
- a guarantee that plural values can always be fully reconciled

Instead, it is an attempt to build a clearer framework for thinking and acting where investment is broader than finance and where the boundary between personal and collective gain remains visible.

## Working difference in one sentence

If many adjacent approaches say:

> wealth is more than money

then Invest-GT adds:

> investment must be evaluated across persons and groups, with visible trade-offs, explicit harms, and a structure that can eventually support operational decision-making.

## Provisional conclusion

Invest-GT belongs near broad-capital, life-design, and systems-oriented traditions, but does not collapse into any one of them.

Its distinctive direction is the combination of:

- broad investment theory
- person/group separation
- visible distribution and collateral damage
- portfolio logic across life domains
- explicit prioritization under constraint
- operational structure for future modeling and testing

That combination is what gives the repository its present identity.
